HEALTH Act

Referred to the House Committee on Rules. ·2025-01-03 ·Official source ↗
Overview

This bill aims to expand access to and lower costs for healthcare services, primarily by permanently extending Affordable Care Act (ACA) subsidies. It also addresses some issues with prescription drug pricing.

Who Is Affected
  • People who buy health insurance through the ACA marketplaces.
  • Individuals and families with moderate incomes.
  • Seniors on Medicare.
  • Pharmaceutical companies.
What Changes
  • The enhanced premium tax credits in the ACA would be made permanent.
  • More people could qualify for financial help with their premiums.
  • Medicare could negotiate prices for some high-cost prescription drugs.
  • There would be a monthly cap on insulin costs for those with Medicare.
What’s Debated
  • Supporters say: This will make health insurance more affordable and cover millions of uninsured Americans.
  • Critics say: Government price negotiation could stifle pharmaceutical innovation and increase drug costs in the long run.
Status

The bill passed the Senate but its status in the House is uncertain as of November 2023.
