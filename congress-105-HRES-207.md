This bill proposes a significant change to how the non-partisan leader of the House’s administrative staff is chosen. Currently, the Speaker of the House appoints the Chief Administrative Officer (CAO), who oversees essential services like security, mail delivery, and technology for all Representatives and their offices. This bill would require the CAO to be elected by the full House of Representatives, rather than appointed by the Speaker.

This change primarily affects Members of Congress and House staff. Representatives will gain a direct say in who leads the administrative branch of the House, potentially increasing accountability. The current CAO and future candidates for the position are also directly affected by this proposed shift in selection process.
