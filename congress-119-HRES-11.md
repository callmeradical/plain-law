OVERVIEW: This bill proposes a change to how Maryland draws its congressional district maps, aiming for more competitive elections and fairer representation. It creates a nine-member independent redistricting commission.

WHO IS AFFECTED:
- Maryland voters in Congressional elections.
- Current politicians who benefit from current districts. 
- Future candidates running for Congress.

WHAT CHANGES:
- Redistricting will be handled by an independent commission.
- The commission’s proposed map would need legislative approval.
- Criteria prioritizing competitive districts and communities are added.

WHAT'S DEBATED:
- Supporters say: This will reduce gerrymandering and give voters more choices.
- Critics say: It could lead to unpredictable outcomes & less accountability.

STATUS: The bill passed the Maryland House of Delegates and is now with the Senate as of February 29, 2024.
