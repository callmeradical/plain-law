OVERVIEW: This bill aims to require federal agencies to use more modern scientific approaches when evaluating the safety of chemicals. It focuses on replacing older testing methods with those considered more accurate and humane. 

WHO IS AFFECTED:
- People exposed to potentially harmful chemicals in everyday products.
- Chemical manufacturers who will need to adapt testing procedures.
- Federal scientists involved in chemical risk assessment.

WHAT CHANGES:
- Agencies must prioritize using non-animal testing methods when available.
- Greater emphasis on real-world exposure data, not just high-dose lab tests. 
- Increased transparency in how chemical safety is determined.

WHAT'S DEBATED:
- Supporters say: This will lead to better protection of public health and reduce animal testing.
- Critics say: Implementing these changes could delay important chemical assessments and increase costs.
