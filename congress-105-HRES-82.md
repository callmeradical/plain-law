This bill proposes a change to how some committees in the U.S. House of Representatives are structured. Currently, committee chairs decide who serves on those committees. This bill would require that the political party with more members in the House have a majority (more than half) of the seats on those committees.

This primarily affects the U.S. House of Representatives and its standing committees – groups of representatives responsible for specific areas like agriculture, education, or foreign affairs. It could also indirectly affect legislation as committee composition influences which bills are considered and how they are shaped. 

Before this bill’s potential passage, committee membership decisions were at the discretion of the committee chairs. After it passes (if it does), the party with more members in the House would have a guaranteed majority on these committees, potentially shifting power dynamics within those groups.
