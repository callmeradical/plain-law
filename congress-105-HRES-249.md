This bill would require that the majority political party (currently Republicans) have a majority of members on each standing committee.

It could also indirectly impact constituents, because committee assignments influence which issues are prioritized in legislation. Lobbyists and interest groups who rely on access to specific lawmakers through committee hearings may also notice a shift.

This means the party controlling the House (currently the Republican Party) would likely control the agenda and outcomes of those committees more directly. Previously, there was often some balance between parties on these committees.
